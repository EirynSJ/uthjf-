What is Combustion?

Combustion is a chemical reaction in which a substance (typically a fuel) reacts rapidly with oxygen, producing heat, light, and various byproducts such as carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O). This reaction releases energy, often in the form of heat and flame.

Types of Combustion

  • Complete Combustion
  • Incomplete Combustion
  • Spontaneous Combustion
  • Rapid Combustion
  • Slow Combustion

Importance and Applications

  • Energy Production
  • Transportation
  • Heating
  • Industrial Uses

Environmental Impact

  • Greenhouse Gases: Combustion of fossil fuels produces carbon dioxide (CO₂) contributes to global warming and climate change.
  • Air Pollution: Incomplete combustion releases harmful pollutants, including carbon monoxide (CO), nitrogen oxides (NOₓ), and particulate matter, leading to air quality problems and health risks.
  • Soot and Smoke: These byproducts can contribute to respiratory issues and environmental degradation.
