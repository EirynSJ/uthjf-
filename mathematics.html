What is Hydroponics?

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil. Instead of soil to support plant growth, hydroponic systems use mediums like perlite, clay pellets, rock wool, or simply water to anchor the plants.

Advantages of Hydroponics

Why Choose Hydroponics?

Hydroponic systems offer multiple advantages, such as faster plant growth, water efficiency, and the ability to grow plants in areas with poor soil quality.

Disadvantages of Hydroponics

The Disadvantages Include:

  • Faster growth: Plants can grow up to 30-50% faster since they receive nutrients directly and efficiently.
  • Space efficiency: Hydroponic systems can be arranged in smaller spaces.
  • Water conservation: Hydroponics typically uses less water, as the water is recirculated within a closed system.
  • Fewer pests and diseases: Since there's no soil, plants are less susceptible to soil-borne diseases and pests.
