Introduction to Hydroponics

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil. Instead of soil, hydroponic systems use mediums like perlite, clay pellets, rock wool, or simply water to anchor the plants.

Materials Required

  • Hydroponics System (e.g., DWC, NFT system, drip system)
  • Container or Grow Tray
  • Net Pots (One net pot for each strawberry plant)
  • Strawberry Plants or Runners
  • Hydroponic Growing Medium (e.g., coconut coir, perlite, rockwool)
  • Hydroponic Nutrients (Recommended Botanicare Sweet Berry for hydro berries.)
  • pH Testing Kit (meter or kit)
  • EC (Electrical Conductivity) Meter
  • LED Grow Lights
  • Thermometer and Hygrometer
  • Pollination Tools (e.g., soft brush)
  • Water Supply
  • Containers for Nutrient Solution

Growing Strawberries Hydroponically

Strawberries grown hydroponically typically take about 60 to 90 days to harvest, depending on the variety and growing conditions.
